Course Content:

This course is divided into 3 Sessions. In these sessions you will cover all the key components of project development using the Scrum Methodology. These sessions are designed to give you a strong understanding of how scrum is implemented in the real world. The sessions will be driven by a pre-defined case study which provides a good example of a real project scenario.

The 3 Sessions will be structured as follows:

Session 1 –

Introduction –

- The course will begin with an introduction into what this course is about, how it will be taught and what you will learn from it.

- An Introduction into Session 1 and Your Learning Objectives

Agile –

A brief history about Agile

- What methodologies were used before Agile existed

- What the Agile movement is

- How Agile is Today

Introduction to the Agile Manifesto

- What the core principles and values mean

The Agile Effect

- What implementing Agile can do

A Quiz regarding Agile

- Scoring enough points will enable you to continue through the course

Scrum –

Introducing Agile Frameworks

- Explaining what Agile Frameworks are

- The most commonly used Frameworks

- Introducing Scrum

A brief History on Scrum

- How Scrum came to be

- How Scrum is used today

What Scrum Values

- What the Scrum Guide is

- What Scrums principles and values are

Scrums Framework

- What Sprints are and what they consist of

- How work is measure in Scrum

- What the Scrum Roles are and what they mean

- What the Scrum Events are

- What other Resources Scrum utilises

An Average Scrum Day

- What each role does on a day-to-day basis

A Quiz regarding Scrum

- Scoring enough points will enable you to continue through the course

Case Study –

Introduction to the Case Study

- What the case study is about

- What your position is in the case study

What you need to do

- Based on the Case Study and Your Position in the Case Study you will be required to create a list of requirements which will be known as the Product Backlog

- A Submission of the Product Backlog will allow you to continue to the next stage

A Quiz regarding the Case Study

- Scoring enough points will enable you to continue through the course

Session 2 –

Introduction –

- An Introduction into Session 2 and what you will complete by the end of the Session

- How the Scrum roles will be utilised throughout the Session

Scrum Event – Sprint Planning Meeting

- Based on the Product Backlog You created in Session 1, you will be asked to analyse, prioritise and define a list of requirements to be implemented during the first Sprint

- You will be following Scrum methods in order to gather these requirements

- You will then submit these requirements in order to continue

Scrum Event – Daily Scrum Meeting

- You will be conducting a daily scrum meeting

- You will base the content of this meeting on the tasks you have been assigned to complete on this sprint

- You will then submit what you discussed during the meeting

Product Design

- This stage is the official start of your First Sprint

- You will be designing some Diagram based on the context of the case study

- You will then be required to submit the diagram(‘s) in order to continue to the next stage

Product Development

- You will begin the first stage of development for the project based on the requirements you gathered

- A submission will be required in order to continue to the next stage

Scrum Event – Sprint Review & Retrospective Meeting

- You will complete and informal review of the progress made during the sprint

- You will then document an assessment of the quality of the sprint based on the requirements, assessing whether the assigned tasks were detailed enough, if there are any new requirements that need to be made and any other issues faces during the sprint

- You will then submit a document in order to conclude the first Sprint

A Quiz regarding Session 2

Session 3 –

Introduction –

- An Introduction into Session 3 and what you will complete by the end of the Session

- How Session 2 will affect the structure of Session 3

- How the Scrum roles will be utilised throughout the Session

Scrum Event – Sprint Planning Meeting

- Based on the Product Backlog You created in Session 1 and the Review/Retrospective Meeting Notes, you will be asked to analyse, prioritise and define a list of requirements to be implemented during the second Sprint

- You will be following Scrum methods in order to gather these requirements

- You will then submit these requirements in order to continue

Scrum Event – Daily Scrum Meeting

- You will be conducting a daily scrum meeting

- You will base the content of this meeting on the tasks you completed on the last sprint followed by the tasks you have been assigned to complete this sprint

- You will then submit what you discussed during the meeting

Product Design

- This stage is the official start of your First Sprint

- You will be designing some Diagram based on the context of the case study

- You will then be required to submit the diagram(‘s) in order to continue to the next stage

Product Development

- You will begin the first stage of development for the project based on the requirements you gathered

- A submission will be required in order to continue to the next stage

Scrum Event – Sprint Review & Retrospective Meeting

- You will complete and informal review of the progress made during the sprint

- You will then document an assessment of the quality of the sprint based on the requirements, assessing whether the assigned tasks were detailed enough, if there are any new requirements that need to be made and any other issues faces during the sprint

- You will then submit a document in order to conclude the first Sprint

A Quiz regarding Session 3

A Review of the Course

- What you have covered in the course

- What you haven’t covered in the course

- Your results in each quiz

- A review of all the documents you created

** Documents created during the sprint will be visible once the Sprint is completed

** Throughout the Process you will be provided examples of how each required document should be structured
